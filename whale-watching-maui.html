Frequently Asked Questions

What's the best month to see whales?
January and February are peak season for humpback whale activity. December through April is when most whales migrate through Hawaiian waters. You can see breaches, tail slaps, and spy hops during these months.
What if we don't see whales?
Our 90% sighting guarantee means we'll refund you if you don't see whales. We know the migration patterns and whale behavior intimately, so we position boats to maximize sightings. In rare no-show cases, you get a full refund or free tour rescheduling.
How close do they come to the boat?
Federal law requires 100 yards minimum distance. Humpbacks are curious though—they often come closer, swim alongside, or jump. With our guide's expertise, you'll see spectacular behaviors without disturbing them.
Is it safe for children?
Yes. We use stabilized boats with covered seating areas. Kids love whale watching—especially when whales breach. We recommend age 3+, and pregnant women should check with their doctor about boat tours.
