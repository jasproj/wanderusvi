Cheapest Helicopter Tours on Big Island

Published April 11, 2026 | ~9 min read

Want to experience Big Island's spectacular volcanic landscapes, waterfalls, and coastlines from the air without breaking the bank? This comprehensive guide reveals how to find the cheapest helicopter tours on Big Island while maintaining excellent value and safety standards.

Understanding Big Island Helicopter Tour Pricing

Big Island helicopter tours range from $300 to $1,000+ per person depending on duration, departure location, and operator. Prices reflect tour length (30 minutes to 2 hours), sightseeing coverage (volcano only vs. full island), and operator overhead. Understanding these factors helps you find legitimate budget options versus poor-value tours.

Cheapest Tour Options by Category

Budget 30-Minute Tours ($300-450)

The most affordable options focus on specific regions like Kilauea Volcano or the Kona coast. These shorter flights still capture stunning views of volcanic terrain, crater formations, and lava flows. Tours departing from Hilo tend to be cheaper due to lower operational costs. Group tours (4-6 passengers) offer better per-person value than private charters. Early morning departures often have cheaper rates than peak afternoon slots.

Mid-Range 45-Minute Tours ($450-650)

This sweet spot offers good value, covering more ground while staying budget-conscious. You'll see multiple volcanic features, waterfalls, and coastal sections. Operators competing in this price range often provide better service and amenities than the absolute cheapest options. Online booking discounts are common here, sometimes dropping prices 10-15% below walk-up rates.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [sunglasses](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Premium Full-Island Tours ($700-1000+)

While more expensive, full-island tours see everything Big Island offers—active volcanos, green sand beaches, waterfalls, and diverse coastlines. Per-minute costs are often comparable to shorter tours when you factor in flight time. Splurging here provides maximum experience and memories.

Money-Saving Strategies

Book Online in Advance

Most operators offer 10-20% discounts for advance online bookings compared to walk-up rates. Booking 1-2 weeks ahead captures these savings and ensures availability. Many tour companies partner with discount travel sites offering flash sales and promotional codes.

Travel During Off-Season

Visiting May-August (rainy season) and September-November brings lower demand and better pricing. While weather can be less predictable, many tours still operate with good visibility. Hotels and car rentals are also cheaper during these periods.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [waterproof bag](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Book Group Tours

Group flights cost 20-30% less per person than private charters. This is the best strategy for budget-conscious travelers. Group tours fill seats more efficiently, reducing operator costs that are passed along as savings.

Combine Deals with Hotel/Car Packages

Many resorts offer discounted helicopter tours as add-ons. Package deals with car rentals and accommodations provide deeper discounts. Package tourism sites like Expedia sometimes bundle tours at better rates.

What to Expect on Budget Tours

Budget tours provide excellent views despite lower prices. You'll see the same volcanic features and landscapes as premium tours, just with shorter flight times or fewer passengers. Helicopter safety is standardized across all FAA-certified operators, so budget doesn't mean compromised safety.

**Pro tip:** Bring a [motion sickness bands](https://amazon.com/dp/B001234567?tag=wandertrav0c1-20) for the best experience.

Avoiding Bad Deals

Never book tours from unlicensed operators or significantly underpriced offerings that seem suspicious. All legitimate tours cost at least $250-300 minimum due to fuel, maintenance, and crew costs. Tours priced far below market rates may indicate safety corner-cutting or misleading descriptions.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much do Big Island helicopter tours cost?

Prices range from $300-800+ per person depending on tour length and operator. Shorter 30-minute tours start around $300-400, while full-island tours can exceed $800. Group discounts, online booking savings, and package deals can reduce costs by 10-20%.

What's the cheapest helicopter tour option?

30-minute tours departing from Hilo typically offer the lowest prices ($300-450). Group tours are cheaper per person than private charters. Booking online, visiting during off-season, and combining with other activities often provides better rates.

Are cheaper helicopter tours less safe?

Not necessarily. All commercial helicopter operators must meet strict FAA safety standards regardless of price. Price differences reflect tour length, views, operator size, and amenities rather than safety standards. Always choose FAA-certified operators with good safety records.

What should I expect on a budget helicopter tour?

Budget tours typically feature shorter flight times (30-45 minutes), basic headset communication, and standard window seating. You'll still see stunning volcanic landscapes, coastlines, and potentially waterfalls. Premium tours offer longer times, better views, and amenities but cost more.
