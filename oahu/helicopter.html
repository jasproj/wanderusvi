Best Helicopter Tours in Oahu

Experience breathtaking aerial views of Diamond Head, Ko'olau Mountains, and dramatic coastlines.

See Oahu from Above

Helicopter tours offer a perspective of Oahu that few visitors get to experience. Soar over Diamond Head, peer into hidden valleys, fly along pristine coastlines, and capture stunning aerial photography. These thrilling adventures are perfect for special occasions, milestone celebrations, or simply unforgettable memories.

Popular Helicopter Routes

Diamond Head Circuit circles Honolulu's most iconic landmark and provides sweeping views of Waikiki and urban Oahu. The Ko'olau Mountain Tour flies deep into windward valleys, showcasing lush cliffs and waterfalls. The Full Island Tour circumnavigates Oahu, covering all regions including the North Shore's famous surf breaks and the isolated West Side.

What to Expect

Tours typically last 30-60 minutes and depart from Honolulu International Airport. Most helicopters accommodate 4-6 passengers with large windows for photography. Expert pilots provide narration and safety briefings. Tours run daily in most weather conditions, though choppy ocean days may affect some routes.

Best Time to Fly

Early morning tours offer the clearest visibility and best light for photography. Mid-day tours provide bright, well-lit conditions. Afternoon flights sometimes encounter trade wind turbulence. Tours operate year-round, with slightly calmer conditions in summer months.

Helicopter Tour FAQs

Are helicopter tours safe?

Yes. Helicopter tour operators in Hawaii maintain strict safety protocols and regular inspections. Pilots are highly trained with thousands of flight hours. Wear your seatbelt and listen to the safety briefing.

Can I take photos from a helicopter?

Absolutely! Most tours are photography-friendly. Bring a camera or use your phone. Ask about door-off photo tours if you want unobstructed shots—some operators offer these premium experiences.

What if I'm afraid of heights?

Many fearful flyers complete helicopter tours successfully. Focus on the amazing views rather than the altitude. Tell your pilot about your concerns—they're experienced with nervous passengers and can help ease your mind.

What's the difference between helicopter tours and airplane tours?

Helicopters fly slower, lower, and can hover over points of interest. Airplanes are less expensive but don't offer the same intimacy or maneuverability. Choose based on your budget and preferences.
